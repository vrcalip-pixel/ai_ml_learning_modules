Ethical Considerations

Education AI Ethics

‚ö†Ô∏è Student Data Privacy

Issue: EdTech platforms collect sensitive data on minors‚Äîtest scores, learning disabilities, behavioral patterns.

Risks: Data breaches, parents unaware what's collected, companies selling student data to advertisers.

Safeguards: FERPA/COPPA regulations, parental consent, data minimization, clear privacy policies

‚öñÔ∏è Bias in Grading & Recommendations

Example: AI essay grader trained on standard English penalizes students who speak dialects or are ESL learners‚Äîeven when ideas are sound.

Another Example: AI recommends easier classes to students from disadvantaged backgrounds, limiting their opportunities.

Solutions: Test AI across diverse student populations, human review of important decisions, transparency about how AI works

üåç Equity & Access

Issue: Wealthy schools afford AI platforms; poor schools don't. Digital divide widens.

Risk: Students with AI assistance learn faster, creating permanent achievement gaps.

Solutions: Government funding for EdTech in underserved schools, free/low-cost AI tools (Khan Academy model), offline alternatives

Marketing AI Ethics

üéØ Manipulation & Dark Patterns

Issue: AI can be TOO good at persuasion‚Äîexploiting psychological triggers to manipulate purchases.

Examples: "Only 2 left in stock!" (artificial scarcity), "Your friend bought this" (social pressure), endless scroll keeping you hooked

Solutions: Ethical design principles, regulations against deceptive practices, consumer education

üîí Privacy Violations

Issue: Tracking across websites, apps, devices to build detailed profiles without consent.

Example: You search "divorce lawyer" ‚Üí Suddenly see ads everywhere. Reveals private information to family using shared devices.

Solutions: GDPR/CCPA regulations, opt-in consent, data deletion rights, tracking transparency

üî¥ Filter Bubbles & Echo Chambers

Issue: AI shows content matching your existing views, reinforcing biases and limiting exposure to diverse perspectives.

Impact: Political polarization, spread of misinformation, inability to understand different viewpoints.

Solutions: Algorithm design that includes diverse perspectives, user controls, media literacy education

üë∂ Predatory Targeting

Issue: AI targets vulnerable groups‚Äîchildren, gambling addicts, people in financial distress.

Example: Payday loan ads shown to people with bad credit searching "need money fast."

Solutions: Regulations prohibiting targeting of vulnerable populations, age restrictions, ethical review boards

üéì Ethical Framework

  • Transparency: Users know when AI is personalizing content
  • Consent: Opt-in, not opt-out, for data collection
  • Fairness: Test for bias, ensure equity across demographics
  • Privacy: Collect only necessary data, secure it properly
  • Human Agency: People control their experience, can override AI
